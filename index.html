Project Aurora

Transforming Patient Appointment Booking at Aurora Health Group

Project Overview & Strategic Context

Aurora Health Group is a mid-sized private healthcare provider delivering services across the UK. With increasing demand for digital access, inefficiencies within the booking journey began to impact patient experience and operational performance.

Project Aurora focused on redesigning the booking process to improve access, reduce inefficiencies, and enable scalable digital service delivery.

  • Reduce booking turnaround time
  • Improve patient experience
  • Reduce DNA rates
  • Enable digital booking

Problem Definition & Research Insights

  • Manual booking processes
  • No real-time availability visibility
  • High admin dependency
  • Inconsistent communication

The issue was not demand, but the lack of integration between demand and clinician capacity.

Current State (AS-IS Process)

The booking process relied heavily on manual coordination, creating delays and inefficiencies.

  • Manual availability checks
  • Repeated patient contact
  • No automation
  • Fragmented systems

Root Cause Analysis

  • No integrated booking platform
  • Lack of centralised availability
  • Manual workflows
  • No automation

Solution Design (TO-BE Model)

  • Digital booking platform
  • Real-time availability
  • Automated reminders
  • Reduced manual effort

Delivery Approach & Methods

  • BPMN process modelling
  • Lean analysis
  • Service design
  • Stakeholder engagement

Business Impact & Metrics

Key Learnings & Reflection

  • Process issues often stem from system fragmentation
  • Demand vs capacity alignment is critical
  • Automation significantly improves efficiency
  • Stakeholder alignment drives success
